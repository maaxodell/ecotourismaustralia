DRIVER GUIDES NEEDED: TASMANIAN SMALL GROUP MULTI-DAY TOURS - UNDER DOWN UNDER TOURS

Our Story:
Under Down Under Tours is the leading operator of active small group tours in Tasmania. Operating since 1996, we are Tasmanian owned and operated, and offer a variety of day and multi-day tour experiences around our state. We have been Advanced Eco Certified since 2002 and are passionate about sharing Tasmania’s wild places and history. Our small group tours focus on nature-based experiences in iconic Tasmanian destinations such as Freycinet, Mt Field and Cradle Mountain National Parks, Bruny Island, the Tasman Peninsula, the West Coast and the Bay of Fires.

Our vision is to create genuine Tasmanian experiences that are respectful, responsible and exceptional, and powered by great people. We aim to deliver life-long memories and friendships for everyone we meet, and enjoy doing it.

The Role:
We operate tours year-round and provide opportunities to work for a season or on a continuing basis. Our Driver Guides are a critical part of our business and are responsible for creating outstanding travelling experiences for our guests. The duration of our tours are varied and range from 1 day to 9 days and can involve walks undertaken with guests of up to 4 hours.

Our Driver Guides are fit, active, passionate about and comfortable in the outdoors. The provision of accurate, entertaining and informative commentary is an important part of the role along with ongoing safe and professional driving skills.

Above all our guides must be considerate and approachable and willing to lead small groups of people from a wide range of backgrounds, ages and cultures.

Job Requirements:
Eligible applicants must have or be willing to obtain the following licenses or qualifications:
Light Rigid license (minimum)
Working with Vulnerable People Accreditation (or equivalent for another Australian State or Territory)
Public Passenger Vehicle License (or equivalent for another Australian State or Territory)
First Aid certification
Full right to work in Australia

Applicants must:
Be able to provide excellent customer service
Have highly developed interpersonal skills
Be highly motivated and confident with working independently
Be a terrific storyteller of local history, culture and nature
Have good personal presentation
Be physically fit and able to walk with guests for up to 4 hours
Have excellent organisational skills
Previous guiding experience is preferred but not essential. A knowledge and love of the outdoors with a passion for the environment is a must.

How to apply:
We are hiring on a rolling basis. If you are interested in the role, we recommend you apply promptly – please email info@underdownunder.com.au with a cover letter and your resume.

COMMUNITY AND VOLUNTEER PROGRAM MANAGER - ACT PARKS AND CONSERVATION SERVICE

The ACT Parks and Conservation Service (PCS) is seeking a people person that believes in the power of community to make a difference. The Community and Volunteer Programs Manager works with community partners to develop, facilitate and evaluate programs that enable community and volunteer engagement in projects throughout the reserve estate.

The position works under limited supervision and is responsible for the delivery of the ParkCare Initiative, including the ParkCare Patch, VisitorAssist, WildlifeAssist and RangerAssist volunteer streams. The position supervises a team of staff and works to support PCS District staff and community partners to engage and promote the involvement of community members in reserve management activities.

Eligibility/Other Requirements:
Mandatory:
Be physically able and willing to undertake incident management duties, including participation in fire standby, fire suppression and fire training.
Be prepared to wear a uniform; and
Possess a manual drivers’ licence.

Prior to commencing this role, a current registration issued under the Working with Vulnerable People (Background Checking) Act 2011 is required. For further information on Working with Vulnerable People registration refer to https://www.accesscanberra.act.gov.au/app/answers/detail/a_id/1804

Highly Desirable:
Tertiary qualifications relevant to Volunteer Management, Natural or Cultural Resource Management and/or Recreation or Tourism Management in protected areas are desirable but not essential.

INTERNSHIPS WITH ECOTOURISM AUSTRALIA

Ecotourism Australia regularly offers internships and volunteer placements to interested candidates and students studying in the fields of tourism management, marketing, eco and sustainable tourism, and environmental science. Our internships and volunteer placements are unpaid, located in Brisbane QLD, and typically require a minimum of three days per week for a minimum duration of three months. Applications for periods of less that 12 weeks will not be considered. To apply for an internship or volunteer placement, please send a cover letter and CV, along with preferred dates for your placement, to Shea Wittig at eco@ecotourism.org.au.
